William Hawkins

Football Coach

Experience

  • Player: I played football throughout my childhood and adolescence, and was scouted by several professional clubs. I played for the youth teams of london United and Sydney fc before a knee injury ended my playing career.
  • Coach: I initially began my coaching career in 2004, starting as a youth coach at Melbourne United. I quickly rose through the ranks and became the head of the academy in 2010. After then I moved to London in 2013 to become the assistant coach of the first team, where I helped lead the team to two National League titles and a Continental League victory. I am currently the head coach of the Nepal national football team.
  • Innovator: I have always been known for my innovative coaching methods, which combine traditional football tactics with cutting-edge sports science. I always have been credited with helping to develop some of the Nepal top players, including Yugal shrestha, Bimal Gharti magar and many others.

Life Outside Football

Off the field, I am a dedicated family man who enjoys spending time with mine wife and two children. I also an avid runner and enjoys hiking in my free time. I also a philanthropist and support several charitable organizations that promote youth sports and education.
